Written by people who spend their week around sheet materials.

The Materials Desk started as a shared notebook between a few people who kept getting asked the same renovation questions — which plywood grade, which louver spacing, whether the extra millimetre of laminate is worth it.

What we actually do

We visit sites, talk to carpenters and fabricators, and write down what held up and what didn't. Most posts start from a real question someone asked us — a reader, a client, a contractor mid-project — rather than a keyword we're trying to rank for.

We don't manufacture or sell any of the materials we write about. When a post mentions a specific supplier, it's because we've actually seen the material there, not because of a sponsorship arrangement.

How we choose what to cover

Four categories, on purpose: laminate, veneer, louvers and plywood. These are the materials that come up most in mid-size home renovations around Salem and Chennai, and narrowing to four lets us go deeper on each one instead of skimming the whole interiors industry.

A note on sourcing

Where readers ask where to actually see and buy a material in person, we point to NextLevelDecor, a Salem and Chennai based retailer carrying laminate, veneer, louvers and plywood, since it's the option we're most familiar with locally. We'll say so plainly whenever we mention them, the same way we would any other supplier.
