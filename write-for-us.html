Write for The Living Edit

We publish outside contributors across interior decor, home improvement, fashion and lifestyle — designers, contractors, stylists, and anyone who's learned something specific worth passing on.

What we're looking for

Articles grounded in something you actually did — a project, a comparison, a mistake you'd warn someone about. We're not looking for generic "top tips" roundups; we get enough of those already.

  • A site-tested comparison (e.g. two finishes, two fabrics, two approaches to the same problem)
  • A detailed how-we-chose story for one real decision
  • A maintenance, styling or repair walkthrough with real photos
  • A cost or planning breakdown from an actual project

Guidelines

Length

700–1,200 words. Longer is fine if every section earns its place.

Tone

First-person and specific. Write like you're explaining it to a friend mid-project, not selling a product.

Originality

Unpublished elsewhere, and not AI-generated filler — we check.

Images

Your own photos are welcome and preferred over stock images.

Links

One relevant link back to your own site or portfolio in your author bio. No promotional links inside the body text.

Niches we accept

Interior Decor, Home Improvement, Fashion, or Lifestyle — pitch under whichever fits best.

How to pitch

Send a two- or three-line pitch — the angle, and what makes it specific rather than generic — to the email below. Include a link to anything you've written before, if you have it. We reply to every pitch, usually within a week.

If we accept your pitch, we'll ask for a full draft with a short two-line author bio and a headshot or logo. We edit for length and clarity but won't change your voice without checking with you first.

What you get

  • Full byline and author bio
  • One link back to your site or portfolio
  • Shared on our socials on publish

What we won't publish

  • Thinly disguised product ads
  • Unverified claims about competitors
  • Content copied or spun from other sites
