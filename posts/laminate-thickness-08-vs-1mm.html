0.8mm vs 1mm laminate: does the extra layer matter?

2026-06-25 · 7 min read

We tracked two identical kitchen shutter sets — same design, same substrate, one finished in 0.8mm laminate and one in 1mm — over eighteen months of daily household use to see whether the extra 0.2mm was worth its premium.

Where the difference showed up

Both sheets looked identical on installation day. The gap appeared at contact points: the 0.8mm shutter picked up small chips along the counter-facing edge within the first six months, while the 1mm edge stayed intact through the same period.

Where it made no difference

Flat surface areas away from edges — wall-facing panels, the inside of a pantry door — showed no meaningful wear difference between the two thicknesses. Scratches from cleaning cloths and daily contact looked the same on both.

Where we'd spend the extra money

  • Countertop-adjacent shutter edges and any surface near a sink
  • High-traffic drawer fronts that see repeated hand contact
  • Anywhere a corner or edge is exposed rather than protected by an edge band

Where we wouldn't bother

Wardrobe interiors, loft shutters, and anything behind a door rarely justify the 1mm premium — the wear that 1mm protects against just doesn't happen there in most homes.

If your budget only stretches to 1mm on some shutters, prioritise it by exposure and contact frequency, not by room.
