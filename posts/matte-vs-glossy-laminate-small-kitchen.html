Matte vs glossy laminate for a small Chennai kitchen

2026-07-02 · 6 min read

A reader renovating a narrow galley kitchen in Chennai asked us to compare matte and glossy laminate on the same shutter design before committing. We photographed both finishes under the kitchen's actual lighting, a week apart, after normal daily use.

What gloss got right — and wrong

Glossy laminate visibly opened up the narrow galley layout, bouncing available light down the length of the room. Within a week, though, fingerprints and water spots near the sink and handles were noticeable under the overhead lighting — the same reflectivity that makes the room feel bigger also shows every mark.

What matte got right — and wrong

Matte hid daily fingerprints and minor splashes almost completely, and needed far less wiping to look presentable. Under the kitchen's warm-toned LED strip lighting, though, it read flatter and slightly duller than expected in photos — a cooler LED tone would likely have suited it better.

Our read for tight kitchens

  • Prioritising light and openness in a narrow layout: gloss, paired with a realistic cleaning routine
  • Prioritising low daily maintenance: matte, paired with slightly cooler-toned lighting than you might default to
  • Undecided: sample both under your kitchen's actual bulbs before ordering — showroom lighting rarely matches home lighting

One thing both finishes agreed on

Handle placement mattered more than finish for fingerprint visibility — shutters with recessed or profile handles picked up far fewer marks than push-open panels, regardless of gloss or matte.
