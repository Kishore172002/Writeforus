BWP or BWR: which plywood grade actually needs to go under your sink

2026-06-02 · 8 min read

Every plywood quote we've reviewed for a Salem or Chennai kitchen has the same line item hiding two very different products: "18mm ply." Whether that sheet is Boiling Water Resistant (BWR) or Boiling Waterproof (BWP) changes how it behaves under a sink for maybe ₹40–70 more per square foot — and most carpenters won't volunteer which one they've quoted unless you ask.

What the grades actually test for

BWR is bonded with phenol formaldehyde resin and tested by boiling a sample for a set period — it survives splashes, humidity and the odd forgotten mop bucket. BWP goes further: longer boiling cycles and a pressure treatment meant to simulate sustained water contact. In plain terms, BWR handles moisture in the air; BWP handles moisture that sits.

Where BWP earns its premium

  • Kitchen sink base units, especially if there's no dedicated waterproof tray underneath
  • Bathroom vanity cabinets and any shutter within splash range of a shower
  • Utility area shelving near a washing machine outlet

Where BWR is genuinely enough

Bedroom wardrobes, TV units, and study furniture rarely see standing water, and paying BWP prices there is money that could go toward better hardware — hinges and channels fail long before BWR ply does in a dry room.

Checking before the sheet gets cut

Look for the ISI mark and grade stamp on the sheet edge, not just the invoice. If your carpenter is sourcing loose sheets rather than pre-marked stock, ask for the manufacturer's batch sticker before it disappears into offcuts. A supplier who can't show you the stamp on request is worth a second question.
