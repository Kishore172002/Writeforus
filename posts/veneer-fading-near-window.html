Why your veneer wardrobe is fading near the window

2026-06-18 · 5 min read

Natural wood veneer ambers and lightens under UV exposure, and it rarely does it evenly. If a wardrobe panel sits within a metre of an unshaded window, expect a visible tone shift within a year or two — the exposed section can end up noticeably lighter or warmer than the rest of the same sheet.

Why book-matched veneer isn't immune

Book-matching (mirroring adjacent grain patterns for symmetry) is about pattern, not UV resistance — a book-matched panel fades exactly as unevenly as a plain one if half of it catches direct sun and half doesn't.

What actually helps

  • A UV-stable lacquer topcoat, applied at the factory rather than added later — reduces the rate of colour shift but doesn't stop it entirely
  • A sheer curtain or film on the nearest window, which cuts direct UV without blocking the room
  • Rotating loose veneer-faced items (like a headboard panel) periodically, where the furniture design allows it

What doesn't work

We've seen a few attempts to "even out" a faded panel with a matched stain — it almost always reads as a visible patch under daylight, because the underlying grain has already changed, not just the surface colour. Prevention is genuinely cheaper than correction here.

If you're choosing veneer today

For a wardrobe or panel that will sit near a window long-term, ask specifically about UV-stable finishing options before ordering, and be honest with yourself about whether that spot gets direct afternoon sun.
